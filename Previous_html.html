Projects


Marsh Bird Data Hub

The Marsh Bird Data Hub is a site created to showcase results and efforts from Audubon Great Lakes' marsh bird monitoring program in Illinois and Indiana. It is an ArcGIS Hub site and includes various tools to engage the public and partner organizations around marsh bird conservation.

Spring Peak Bird Migration

This map was created as part of a larger project that's looking to identify priority areas for bird habitat conservation along a portion of Lake Michigan's eastern coast. Nexrad radar data was used to identify areas important for migratory birds within this focus area. This map was made using ArcGIS Pro.

Parks & Income

A quick look at Lake County Forest Preserves and Illinois State Parks locations and income in Lake County, IL. Maps were made in CARTO.

Illinois Waterways

This map depicts the waterways of Illinois categorized by Strahler stream order. It was made using QGIS.

Recreation & Amenities - Lake County, IL

This map highlights amenities (restaurants, bars, libraries, etc.) within a two-mile radius of the parks and preserves in central Lake County, IL. It was made in QGIS

Black Tern Priority Sites

Black Terns are gull-like marsh birds that have declined drastically in North America. This map depicts known Black Tern nesting locations in most of Michigan's Upper Peninsula, current and past. It was made using ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Online Web AppBuilder.
